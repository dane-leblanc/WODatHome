Workout Types

AMRAP

AMRAP is an acronym for "As Many Rounds as Possible". They involve doing as many repetitions of a series of exercises as possible during a set amount of time. You will need to build multiple stages with the same number of different exercises in each stage. You can also customize the amount of rest you get between stages. Be sure to log how many rounds you were able to get through for each stage (be sure to include how many reps you made it into your final round).

EMOM

EMOM is an acronym for "Every Minute On the Minute". These workouts challenge you to complete an exercise for a certain number of reps in less than 60 seconds. Any remaining will serve as recovery time before you move on to the next exercise. If you select fewer exercises than there are minutes in your workout, the exercises will just cycle back from the beginning until your time is up.

RFT

RFT is an acronym for "Rounds For Time". This workout asks you to complete a specified number of rounds of a set of exercises as quickly as possible. Keep track of how quickly you can get through and try to beat your highest logged time!
